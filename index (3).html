Data Center Resource Effectiveness (DCRE) Metric — Technical Reference & Scoring Methodology
Document Ref: MeitY-DCRE-METH-2024-001 · Version 1.0 · Effective Date: January 1, 2024 · Ministry of Electronics & Information Technology, Government of India · Supersedes: PUE Reference Guide v3.2
1. Facility Energy Effectiveness (FEE)▼
Definition

Facility Energy Effectiveness (FEE) is the ratio of total facility energy consumed to IT equipment energy consumed. FEE is equivalent to PUE in format (≥1.0, lower is better) and serves as the primary energy efficiency indicator within the DCRE framework.

Formula
FEE = Total Facility Energy (kWh) ÷ Total IT Equipment Energy (kWh)
Data Inputs

Total Facility Energy: All energy consumed at the facility boundary, including IT load, cooling, lighting, and power conversion losses.
IT Equipment Energy: Energy consumed by compute, storage, and networking equipment directly serving the data center mission.

Measurement Period

Annual measurement is required. Data must reflect 12 consecutive months of operation. Partial-year submissions require a proportional extrapolation methodology with documented justification. FEE cannot be below 1.0 by definition.

2. Water Consumed & Water Usage Effectiveness (WUE)▼
Definition

Water Usage Effectiveness (WUE) measures the volume of potable water consumed at the facility per unit of IT energy consumed. WUE is applicable for DCRE Level 1 assessments only.

Potable Water Scope

For purposes of this metric, "water consumed" means potable water: water derived from municipal water systems or fresh water withdrawn from aquifers suitable for human consumption or farming. The following are excluded from the water consumed measurement:

  • Water drawn from a body of water and returned directly (not consumed)
  • Internally recycled water within site boundaries
  • Collected rainwater used within the site boundary
Formula
WUE = Annual Water Consumed (liters) ÷ Annual IT Energy (kWh)
3. Water Stress Factor (WS)▼
Source

The Water Stress Factor is derived from the World Resources Institute (WRI) Aqueduct Water Risk Atlas. Geographic determination is based on the facility's registered latitude and longitude coordinates cross-referenced against the Aqueduct Baseline Water Stress layer.

Scale Descriptions (0–5)

0Low<10% of available water withdrawn annually
1Low-Medium10–20% of available water withdrawn

4. Water Usage Impact (WUI)▼
Definition

Water Usage Impact (WUI) is a composite proxy metric that incorporates both the volume of water consumed and the scarcity context of the geographic location. WUI replaces WUE at DCRE Levels 2 through 4 to provide a more operationally relevant indicator of water resource impact.

Formula
WUI = Water Consumed (liters/kWh) × (Water Stress Factor + 1)

The additive constant of 1 ensures WUI is defined even in zero-stress regions, maintaining consistent mathematical behavior across all stress levels. A facility in a zero-stress zone (WS=0) still accrues a WUI equal to its WUE, ensuring consumption is never fully discounted.

5. Climate Zones (ASHRAE 169)▼
Basis

Climate Zone factors are derived from ASHRAE Standard 169 (Climatic Data for Building Design Standards), adapted for data center thermal management assessment. The scale runs from 1 (hardest to cool) to 7 (easiest to cool), reflecting the thermodynamic effort required for heat rejection.

Why DCRE Replaces PUE

The Data Center Resource Effectiveness metric was developed in response to six identified limitations of PUE as a standalone efficiency benchmark. Each criterion reflects a quality standard adopted under MeitY's National Data Centre Policy framework for regulatory and compliance use across India:

1. Ease of Use: DCRE is computed from operationally available data already collected by most modern data centres in India. No additional instrumentation is required at Levels 1–2, easing adoption for Tier II and Tier III cities.
2. Cost: Implementation cost is negligible at lower levels, leveraging existing utility metering and water billing data rather than specialised submetering infrastructure — aligning with India's Digital Infrastructure expansion targets.
3. Non-Invasiveness: DCRE does not require physical access to IT equipment or network topology disclosure, preserving security and operational confidentiality for government and defence-adjacent data centre facilities.
4. Objectivity: Geographic water stress factors are sourced from a neutral third-party (WRI Aqueduct), eliminating self-reported bias and ensuring consistent cross-facility comparability across India's diverse hydro-climatic regions.
5. Modelling Capability: The tiered level structure enables progressive adoption and scenario modelling under MeitY's Green Data Centre guidelines, allowing facilities to project tier changes before making capital investments.
6. Relevance: By incorporating water stress geography and climate zone penalties specific to the Indian subcontinent, DCRE captures the full environmental cost of data centre operations in a single composite score, directly aligned with India's National Water Policy and Net Zero commitments.
